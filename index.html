The Earth Illusion
Make reality feel weird.
Then make it make sense.
A precise, living demonstration of perception: a static image that many viewers feel rotate when they scan it. No animation. Just your brain reading gradients as motion.
Tip: Don’t stare at one spot. Let your eyes wander or gently scroll; most people see slow rotation in the rings. If you don’t, step back a little or dim the screen slightly.

What you’re perceiving

Peripheral drift illusion. Alternating segments with a specific luminance order (dark → cool → light → warm) bias motion detectors in your visual cortex. When your eyes make micro-movements, the pattern is misread as rotation.

This is the Earth Illusion in miniature: form + position drive truth in perception. Change the order (form) or your gaze (position), and the world behaves differently—without the world changing.

Use it

When a problem feels stuck, change the sampling: posture, angle, or pace. Your perception is a moving camera—small shifts unlock new affordances. That’s perceptual stewardship.
