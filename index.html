Focus Trap — 60s

Seconds until colors faded: —
Instructions: Sit still. Stare at the center dot for sixty seconds without moving your eyes. Many people report the corner colors vanishing. That’s retinal stabilization (Troxler fading) + attention filtering. Log your time, then scroll for the why.

What just happened?

Your eyes don’t stream the world; they sample it. When an image stays still on your retina, neurons stop firing as much. Your brain reallocates bandwidth to changes (what might matter) and quietly lets the stable stuff melt into the background.

We call this a piece of the Earth Illusion: attention is an editor. The goal of this channel is to turn that knowledge into daily leverage— better focus, kinder arguments, cleaner self‑talk.

Try the applied version: pick one recurring stressor. When it shows up today, freeze for one breath and ask, “What am I not sampling?” Then shift one variable—location, posture, or question—and resample. That’s a micro‑coherence reset.
